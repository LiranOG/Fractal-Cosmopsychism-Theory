§1   Motivation & Overview

No analytical tool currently exists to compute the full observable spectrum — gravitational waves, electromagnetic burst, neutrino fluence, and recoil velocity — for the coherent radial coalescence of N supermassive black holes from a single, unified theoretical framework. Numerical relativity handles at most 2–3 bodies; post-Newtonian expansions lose validity near the horizon; perturbation theory cannot capture the strong-field regime.

PRISM bridges this gap by combining three ideas:

  1. Dihedral symmetry reduction — the initial DN symmetry of a regular polygon collapses the N-body force law into a single effective two-body problem, with a closed-form geometric factor $s_N$.
  2. Group-theoretic multipole classification — gravitational radiation is decomposed into irreducible representations of DN; only the totally-symmetric A1 mode contributes to leading-order emission, giving a rigorous suppression law.
  3. Effective Horizon Functional (EHF) — a novel construct that maps the multi-body infall trajectory to a single scalar functional on phase space, from which final mass, spin, and recoil follow by differentiation.

All formulas are closed-form and immediately computable. The interactive explorer below evaluates them in real time.

§2   Initial Configuration

$N$ equal-mass SMBHs sit at the vertices of a regular $N$-gon of circumradius $R_0$ in the $xy$-plane. A central mass $M_c$ (stellar cluster) occupies the origin. By $\mathrm{D}_N$ symmetry, all tangential forces cancel; each body accelerates purely radially inward.

§3   Mathematical Core

3.1 Effective mass and equation of motion

The chord from vertex 0 to vertex $k$ is $d_k = 2R\sin(\pi k/N)$. Summing the radial components of all inter-body and central-mass forces and defining the geometric shape factor:

EQ 1 $$s_N = \frac{1}{4}\sum_{k=1}^{N-1}\csc\!\left(\frac{\pi k}{N}\right)$$

the effective mass driving each body's radial infall is

EQ 2 $$M_{\rm eff} = M_c + m\,s_N, \qquad \ddot{R} = -\frac{GM_{\rm eff}}{R^2}$$

Energy conservation from rest at $R_0$:

EQ 3 $$\dot{R} = -\sqrt{2GM_{\rm eff}\!\left(\frac{1}{R}-\frac{1}{R_0}\right)}$$

3.2 Group-theoretic radiation decomposition

The mass quadrupole tensor $I_{ij}$ of a regular $N$-gon belongs to the $A_1 \oplus E_2$ representation of $\mathrm{D}_N$. For $N \geq 3$ the $E_2$ (off-diagonal) sector vanishes by symmetry, and only the $A_1$ (breathing) mode contributes to leading-order emission. This gives the symmetry-suppressed quadrupole:

EQ 4 $$I_{xx}=I_{yy}=\tfrac{1}{6}M_{\rm ring}R^2, \quad I_{zz}=-\tfrac{1}{3}M_{\rm ring}R^2$$

where $M_{\rm ring} = Nm$. Higher multipoles (hexadecapole $\ell=4$) contribute at order $(v/c)^2$ beyond the quadrupole and are neglected here.

3.3 Gravitational-wave power and integrated energy

Applying the quadrupole formula $P_{\rm GW} = (G/5c^5)\sum_{ij}(\dddot{I}_{ij})^2$ with $\dddot{I}_{ij}$ evaluated via Eq. 3:

EQ 5 $$P_{\rm GW}(R) = \frac{2}{15}\frac{G^3}{c^5} M_{\rm ring}^2 M_{\rm eff}^2 \frac{\dot{R}^2}{R^4}$$

Integrating from $R_0$ to the common apparent horizon $R_{\rm AH} = 2GM_{\rm tot}/c^2$ and evaluating the Beta-function integral (see Appendix A of the companion paper):

EQ 6 — CENTRAL RESULT $$\boxed{E_{\rm GW} = \frac{1}{210} \frac{M_{\rm ring}^2\,M_{\rm eff}^{5/2}}{M_{\rm tot}^{7/2}}\,c^2 \left[1-\!\left(\frac{R_{\rm AH}}{R_0}\right)^{7/2}\right]}$$

3.4 Peak GW luminosity and characteristic frequency

EQ 7 $$\tau_{\rm merge} \simeq \frac{R_{\rm AH}}{c}, \qquad L_{\rm GW}^{\rm peak} \simeq \frac{E_{\rm GW}}{\tau_{\rm merge}}, \qquad f_{\rm peak} = \frac{c}{2\pi R_{\rm AH}}$$

3.5 Effective Horizon Functional (EHF) — final mass & spin

PRISM introduces the Effective Horizon Functional: a scalar $\mathcal{F}$ defined on the space of initial data $\{N, m, M_c, R_0, \{\chi_i\}, \{\delta_i\}\}$ whose partial derivatives yield the key observables.

EQ 8 $$\mathcal{F} = M_{\rm tot}\,c^2 - E_{\rm GW} - E_{\rm EM}$$
EQ 9 $$M_{\rm final} = \frac{\mathcal{F}}{c^2}, \qquad a_{\rm lower} = \frac{\bar\chi}{N^{3/2}}, \qquad a_{\rm final} \approx 0.2\text{–}0.7 \ (\text{with orbital perturbations})$$

3.6 Recoil velocity

In the symmetric case, recoil cancels exactly. For perturbation amplitude $\delta_R/R_0$, the recoil velocity from asymmetric mass distribution scales as (Campanelli et al. 2007):

EQ 10 $$v_{\rm recoil} \simeq 1200\ \mathrm{km\,s^{-1}} \times \frac{\delta_R}{R_0} \times \left(\frac{N-1}{N}\right)^2 \eta^2 \Delta\chi$$

where $\eta = \mu/M_{\rm tot}$ is the symmetric mass ratio and $\Delta\chi$ is the spin asymmetry. For the symmetric case $\delta_R=0$: $v_{\rm recoil}=0$.

3.7 Electromagnetic burst

Tidal disruption of the central stellar mass produces a polar outflow. The ejection fraction scales geometrically as $f_{\rm ej} \approx R_*/R_{\rm AH}$ (Guillochon & Ramirez-Ruiz 2013), and the shock temperature:

EQ 11 $$T_{\rm shock} \approx \left(\frac{3\rho_* v_{\rm ff}^2} {4\,a_{\rm rad}}\right)^{1/4}, \qquad E_{\rm EM} = \eta_{\rm EM}\cdot f_{\rm ej}\cdot M_* c^2$$

with $\eta_{\rm EM} \approx 5\times10^{-3}$ (uncertain by $\pm 1$ dex; requires GRMHD simulation to constrain).

3.8 Neutrino fluence

At $T_{\rm shock} \sim 4\,\mathrm{MeV}$, pair processes dominate over hadron interactions. The neutrino energy fraction of the shock luminosity is $f_\nu \approx 0.03$–$0.10$ for optically-thick shocked plasma (Ruffini et al. 2010):

EQ 12 $$E_\nu \approx f_\nu \cdot E_{\rm EM} \cdot \left(\frac{T_{\rm shock}}{4\,\mathrm{MeV}}\right)^2$$

§7   Scaling Laws from DN Symmetry

A key output of PRISM is a set of power-law scaling relations derived from the DN representation theory:

$$E_{\rm GW} \propto \frac{N^{2}\,s_N^{5/2}(N)}{N^{7/2}} \cdot M_{\rm tot}c^2 \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; \varepsilon_{\rm GW} \approx \frac{s_N^{5/2}}{210\,N^{3/2}}$$

This shows that $\varepsilon_{\rm GW}$ is non-monotonic in $N$: it peaks near $N \approx 6$–$8$ before declining as $s_N \sim N\ln N$ grows slower than $N^{3/2}$. For large $N$, $s_N \to (N/\pi)\ln N$ and $\varepsilon_{\rm GW} \to ({\ln N})^{5/2} / (210\,\pi^{5/2}\,N^{1/2})$, demonstrating that adding more black holes decreases per-body efficiency — a direct consequence of the DN suppression of the non-axisymmetric radiation modes.

$$\tau_{\rm merge} \propto M_{\rm tot}, \qquad f_{\rm peak} \propto M_{\rm tot}^{-1}, \qquad E_{\rm EM} \propto M_*\left(\frac{R_*}{M_{\rm tot}}\right)$$

§8   Limitations & Future Work

L1
Newtonian dynamics. Post-Newtonian corrections exceed 10% below $10\,R_{\rm AH}$. Full NR is required for the final merger phase.
L2
Perfect DN symmetry. No astrophysical process produces a perfect regular polygon. Symmetry breaking increases GW emission toward the binary limit.
L3
EM efficiency uncertain. $\eta_{\rm EM} = 5\times10^{-3}$ carries $\pm 1$ dex uncertainty; GRMHD+radiation-transport required.
L4
No spin dynamics during infall. Spin-orbit and spin-spin couplings are omitted (1.5PN and 2PN effects, subdominant for $v\ll c$).
L5
EHF is an effective construct. The Effective Horizon Functional is a phenomenological parameterisation, not derived from first-principles GR.

Future extensions: (i) 1PN-corrected inspiral with radiation-reaction; (ii) hexadecapole corrections for large $N$; (iii) full NR validation for $N=3$, 4; (iv) GRMHD simulation of the polar outflow; (v) LISA detectability estimates for $z\leq 5$.
